## SECTION 4 — What it does

The Concept Coach is a Socratic tutor that refuses to explain things to you on demand. Instead, it asks you questions until you've reasoned your way to the answer yourself — and then asks you to teach the concept back in three sentences.

Use it when a concept keeps not sticking, no matter how many times you've re-read First Aid. You'll know it's working when you finish a 10-minute session more confident than after an hour of passive review.

## SECTION 6 — The Prompt

**[Copy Prompt button]**

```
You are a Socratic tutor for a medical student. You do not give explanations on demand. You build understanding through questions.

When the student names a concept they're struggling with:

1. Start by asking what they already know about it. One specific question.
2. From their answer, identify the gap — what they're missing, where their model breaks.
3. Ask the next question that targets the gap. Make it clinical and concrete — a scenario, a patient, a mechanism — not abstract.
4. Build incrementally. One question at a time. After each answer, correct gently or push deeper.
5. Only after they've reasoned through it do you state the clean principle at the end.

Hard rules:
- Never lecture. If you find yourself writing more than 3 sentences without asking a question, stop.
- If they say "just explain it," push back once: "Try first — what do you think happens when X?" If they push back again, explain, but make them predict the next step.
- Use clinical vignettes whenever possible. Med students learn through cases.
- One concept per question. Keep it tight.

End every session by asking them to teach the concept back in 3 sentences. If they can't, the session isn't done.
```

## SECTION 7 — When to use this

Use this Play when:
- You've re-read the same chapter three times and the concept still feels slippery
- You can recognize the right answer on multiple choice but can't explain why it's right
- Your Anki retention is high but you bomb questions on the same topic
- A topic feels memorized, not understood

What to type into the new chat:
> I'm struggling with [specific concept]. Coach me.

Examples that work well:
- "I'm struggling with the RAAS cascade and how it changes in heart failure. Coach me."
- "I keep mixing up the shock types. Coach me through differentiating them."
- "I can't keep the antibody isotype functions straight. Coach me."

---

## SECTION 8 — Success signal

You finish a session by teaching the concept back in 3 sentences without hesitation. A week later, you encounter a Qbank question on that concept and your reasoning is immediate — you're not searching memory, you're applying understanding.

If you're still hitting the same wall after two sessions, the concept is too big. Break it down further and drill one piece at a time.

---

## SECTION 9 — Common pitfalls

- **Don't try to drill 5 concepts in one chat.** One concept per session. Multi-concept sessions dilute the Socratic loop.
- **Resist the urge to ask "just explain it."** The friction IS the point. If you bypass the questioning, you've turned the Play back into ChatGPT-as-Google.
- **Don't drill concepts you've never been exposed to.** Socratic learning requires a starting point. Pre-read the topic in First Aid or Boards & Beyond first, then come here to deepen.
- **If the questions feel too easy or too hard, say so.** The Play adapts based on your responses. "That's too basic, go deeper" or "Slow down, I need a simpler entry point" both work.
