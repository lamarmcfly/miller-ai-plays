## Section 1 — Welcome and context (5 min)

**Script:**

> Thanks for being here. Quick context before we dive in.
>
> Medical students are using AI tools — but mostly badly. They treat ChatGPT like Google, they don't know how to write a system prompt, and they won't read a 20-page guide on prompt engineering. AES needs to close that gap, but if I'm the one running the resource, it dies the moment my bandwidth shifts. So we're doing this differently.
>
> Miller AI Plays is going to be a peer-curated library of 60-to-90-second AI workflows. The methodology and the founding five Plays come from AES — that's my work. Everything after that gets built and maintained by you. The three of you are the founding Council.
>
> Here's why I asked each of you specifically: [give each member their one-line, individually. Real and specific. No generic compliments.]
>
> Today we're going to: sign the Charter, elect a Chair, assign your first Plays, and walk through the actual site. By the end of this hour, you're operational.

---

## Section 2 — Charter walk-through and signing (15 min)

**Script:**

> You've all read the Charter. I want to spend ten minutes on the four sections that matter most, then five minutes on questions and signing.

### Critical sections to highlight

**Section 8 — Time commitment**
> Two to three hours a month, plus four to six hours per Play you author. Roughly 30–40 hours over the academic year. If that's not doable, tell me now. Empty seat is better than no-show seat.

**Section 9 — Benefits**
> AES service credit, LOR from me for members in good standing, permanent authorship credit on Plays you write, co-author consideration on any research that comes out of this. The LOR is real and substantive — I've been writing them for 13 years.

**Section 10 — Editorial standards**
> The eight standards are the quality bar. Standards 5 and 6 — no academic integrity violations and no clinical decision-making — are the institutional protection. Don't water those down.

**Section 13 — Intellectual property**
> Read this one carefully. The framework stays with AES. Plays you write are credited to you permanently. You can put authored Plays on your CV. What you can't do is take the library externally or commercialize it — that path runs through AES via GrayMar.

### Q&A and signing

> Questions before we sign?
>
> [Pause. Answer honestly. If you don't know, say so.]
>
> If you're in, sign now. If you have hesitation, raise it. There's no penalty for saying not now.

**[Members sign their copies. You countersign.]**

## Section 3 — Chair election (10 min)

**Script:**

> The Chair runs the monthly meetings, manages the submission queue, and has final editorial sign-off. One academic year, non-renewable consecutively.
>
> I have a strong opinion on who should be Chair, but I'm going to keep it to myself until you discuss it. The Council picks its own Chair.
>
> Take five minutes among yourselves. I'll step out. When I come back, tell me who.

**[Step out. Return after 5 min.]**

> Who's the Chair?
>
> [Confirm. Congratulate. Move on.]

**Notes for Lamar:**
- Your strongest MS3 or MS4 should be Chair — credibility across cohorts, still at Miller for the full founding year, residency-ready writing skills
- If the group picks someone other than your preferred choice, accept it — they have to work together for a year
- Only override if the elected Chair has a clear disqualifier (academic standing concern, graduating in fall, etc.)

## Section 4 — First Play assignments (10 min)

**Script:**

> I'm authoring the founding five Plays. They'll be live in the next two weeks. They are:
>
> 1. Error Engine — Qbank error analysis
> 2. Lecture Compressor — lecture notes to Anki cards
> 3. Concept Coach — Socratic tutor for sticky concepts
> 4. Shelf Review Notebook — NotebookLM for shelf prep
> 5. OSCE Encounter Sim — patient encounter practice
>
> What I need from each of you is one Play, authored by you, published within four weeks. Council members eating their own dog food before we open the doors to the broader student body.
>
> The brief: pick a workflow you actually use or wish existed. It must solve a problem a Miller student has this semester. Use the template. Use the editorial standards. Submit it via the Submissions List.
>
> Take five minutes. Each of you commit out loud to a topic before you leave this room.

## Section 6 — Commitments, calendar, close (5 min)

**Script:**

> Three things before we close.
>
> One — each of you, say out loud what you're committing to today. Topic, deadline, time commitment.
>
> Two — pull out your calendars right now. Block the monthly Friday meetings for the rest of the academic year. Block one hour per week as Play authoring time. If it's not on your calendar, it doesn't happen.
>
> Three — the public launch is gated on you authoring those first three Plays. Until that happens, we're not opening the doors. The pressure is real but it's also the reason this works — when students see your names on the Plays, they take it seriously.
>
> I'm proud of you for being in this room. Let's build something good.

**[Close. Distribute signed Charter copies. Schedule the first monthly meeting before anyone leaves.]**
