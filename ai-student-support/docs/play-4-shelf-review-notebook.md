## SECTION 4 — What it does

NotebookLM is Google's research notebook tool that only answers questions using sources you give it. Unlike ChatGPT or Claude, it won't make things up — every answer cites which source it came from.

This Play walks you through building a shelf-prep notebook for any clerkship: load it with your trusted sources (UWorld notes, Anking, OnlineMedEd transcripts, study guides), then use it as an interactive review partner that grounds every answer in material you've already vetted.

Use it 2–3 weeks before a shelf exam. You'll know it's working when you stop second-guessing whether an answer came from a trusted source or an AI hallucination.

---

## SECTION 5 — How to set it up

This Play has more setup than the others, but you only do it once per shelf.

**Step 1 — Open NotebookLM**
- Go to **notebooklm.google.com**
- Sign in with your Google account (personal or UM, both work)

**Step 2 — Create the notebook**
- Click **+ Create new notebook**
- Name it exactly: `Shelf — [Subject]` (e.g., `Shelf — Internal Medicine`)

**Step 3 — Load sources (this is the key step)**
NotebookLM accepts up to 50 sources per notebook. Curate carefully — quality over quantity.

Strong source candidates:
- Your own notes (paste as text or upload as PDF/Google Doc)
- OnlineMedEd / Boards & Beyond transcripts (export as text)
- High-yield review documents (Step Up to Medicine summaries, Pestana, etc. if you own them)
- Wiki-style review documents (AnkiHub notes, your school's clerkship handbook)
- Past shelf practice question explanations you've curated

What NOT to load:
- Copyrighted Qbank content (NBME, UWorld) — don't redistribute these
- Random web pages (NotebookLM works best with curated, dense sources)
- Personal patient notes (FERPA and HIPAA issues)

**Step 4 — Customize the notebook's behavior**
Once sources are loaded, click the **Notebook guide** panel on the right. NotebookLM will auto-generate a summary, study guide, briefing doc, and FAQ. Skim these to confirm your sources cover what you need.

**Step 5 — Start asking it questions**
The chat interface is at the bottom. Every answer cites the source.

## SECTION 6 — The Prompt

NotebookLM doesn't use system prompts the way Claude does. Instead, structure your **questions** using the patterns below.

**Copy these prompt patterns:**

```
PATTERN 1 — Topic deep dive
"Based on my sources, give me a high-yield summary of [topic] for the shelf exam. Include: mechanism, presentation, diagnosis, treatment, and 3 board-favorite associations."

PATTERN 2 — Differential generation
"A patient presents with [chief complaint]. Based on my sources, what's the differential and how do I narrow it down clinically?"

PATTERN 3 — Compare-and-contrast
"Compare [Disease A] vs [Disease B] across pathophysiology, presentation, key labs, and treatment. Use only what's in my sources."

PATTERN 4 — Practice question generation
"Generate 5 NBME-style shelf practice questions on [topic]. Use only content from my sources. Include answer rationales."

PATTERN 5 — Pre-rounding prep
"Tomorrow I'm seeing patients with [condition]. From my sources, give me the 5 things I need to know to look prepared on rounds: presentation, key labs, first-line workup, first-line treatment, and what attendings love to pimp on."

PATTERN 6 — Gap identification
"I just missed this UWorld question: [paste question + your answer + correct answer]. Based on my sources, what concept did I misunderstand?"
```

## SECTION 7 — When to use this

Use this Play:
- **3 weeks before a shelf:** build the notebook, load sources, start daily 15-min sessions
- **The night before rounds:** Pattern 5 (pre-rounding prep)
- **After every UWorld block:** Pattern 6 (gap identification grounded in your trusted sources, not AI hallucination)
- **When studying with classmates:** share the notebook, study from the same source base

Update the notebook as you go. New high-yield material? Add it. Outdated source? Remove it. The notebook is a living artifact.

## SECTION 9 — Common pitfalls

- **Don't load 50 random web pages.** NotebookLM's quality is bounded by your sources. Load 10 great sources, not 50 mediocre ones.
- **Don't upload copyrighted Qbank content.** NBME, UWorld, AMBOSS — their terms prohibit redistribution. Use your OWN notes from those resources instead.
- **Don't share notebooks with patient information.** FERPA + HIPAA. Even de-identified patient stories in your notes should be scrubbed before loading.
- **Don't treat citations as ground truth.** NotebookLM cites which source it pulled from, but it doesn't verify the source itself is correct. If your sources have errors, the notebook will repeat them.
- **Build one notebook per shelf, not one mega-notebook.** Cross-subject notebooks dilute the focus. Internal Medicine and Surgery deserve separate notebooks.
