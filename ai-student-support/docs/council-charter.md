## 1. Purpose

The Miller AI Plays Council exists to maintain and grow a peer-curated library of AI workflows ("Plays") that help medical students at the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine study smarter, perform better, and integrate emerging tools into their training.

This Charter defines how the Council operates, who serves on it, and the standards by which Plays are published.

## 2. Background

Miller AI Plays was founded in 2026 by Academic Enrichment Services to address a clear gap: medical students are aware of AI tools but lack practical, student-tested workflows for using them well. The Council exists because the only people who truly know what medical students need are medical students — and the only way to keep this resource alive long-term is to put it in student hands.

AES retains the underlying methodology and the founding five Plays. The Council operates and grows the library on behalf of the student body.

## 3. Mission

To deliver short, immediately usable AI workflows that medical students can adopt in under five minutes — and to maintain the highest standard of practical, evidence-informed, student-respecting content.

## 4. Council composition

- **Size:** 3 to 5 active members at any time
- **Cohort representation target:** at minimum one member from MS3 or MS4 (clinical years), plus members from MS1 and MS2 (preclinical) as available
- **Founding Council (2026):** seated by AES in consultation with the first members
- **Subsequent Councils:** seated by the outgoing Council with AES advisor input

## 5. Roles & responsibilities

### 5.1 Chair (one member)

- Coordinates monthly meetings and sets the agenda
- Serves as primary point of contact with AES
- Maintains the Play submission queue
- Has final editorial sign-off on published Plays (with AES advisor input on methodology-sensitive content)
- Term: one academic year, non-renewable consecutively

### 5.2 Members

- Review submitted Plays against the editorial standards (Section 10)
- Author at least one new Play during their term
- Maintain at least two existing Plays (review, update, retire as needed)
- Attend monthly Council meetings
- Recruit and onboard their successor at term end

### 5.3 AES advisor (Lamar Martin or designee)

- Holds office hours for the Council twice per month
- Maintains the methodology and the founding five Plays
- Has veto authority on Plays that conflict with academic integrity, accreditation, or institutional policy
- Provides letters of recommendation and service verification for members in good standing
- Does NOT manage day-to-day operations or attend every meeting

## 6. Term length & rotation

- Standard term: one academic year (August through May)
- Members may serve up to two consecutive terms
- Rotation is staggered: not all seats turn over at once. At least one returning member carries institutional memory into the next Council.

## 7. Selection process

- Outgoing Council solicits applications from the broader student body each spring
- Selection criteria: demonstrated interest in AI tools or learning science, evidence of follow-through, cohort representation, peer respect
- Outgoing Council shortlists; AES advisor confirms; new members onboarded in May for August start
- Founding Council exception: members seated directly by AES advisor for 2026 academic year only

## 8. Time commitment

Be honest with yourself before accepting a seat. This is the realistic ask:

- 2–3 hours per month for review and meetings
- 4–6 hours one-time investment to author a Play
- Surge work during launch periods or major content refreshes

Total estimate: ~30–40 hours per academic year. If you cannot commit this, decline the seat. Better to have an empty seat than a no-show.

## 9. Member benefits

- Service credit through AES (documented for residency applications)
- Letter of recommendation from the AES advisor for members in good standing
- Authorship credit on all Plays you write (visible on the page, permanent)
- Co-authorship consideration on any research, conference presentations, or publications that result from Miller AI Plays
- Direct mentorship from AES on AI tools, learning science, and academic medicine career paths
- Documented leadership experience for CV and residency interviews

## 10. Editorial standards

Every published Play must satisfy all eight criteria below. No exceptions.

1. **Solves a real problem.** The Play addresses a specific, named workflow medical students actually do (study, review, simulate, document, etc.).
2. **Under 90 seconds to demo.** If you cannot show the value in 90 seconds of screen recording, the Play is too complex. Break it up or kill it.
3. **One tool, one job.** Each Play uses one primary AI tool (Claude, Copilot, NotebookLM, etc.) for one defined purpose. Cross-tool workflows are v2 territory.
4. **Tested on real student work.** The author must have used the Play on their own coursework and document the outcome.
5. **No academic integrity violations.** Plays must not help students misrepresent their work, bypass assessments, or violate any UM policy. When in doubt, ask the AES advisor.
6. **No clinical decision-making.** Plays do not generate diagnoses, treatment plans, or anything that could be confused with clinical reasoning on real patients.
7. **Tool-agnostic where possible.** Prompts should work across Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot, etc., unless the Play specifically depends on one tool's feature (e.g., NotebookLM sourcing).
8. **Last-reviewed within 90 days.** AI tool UIs change. If a Play hasn't been reviewed in 90 days, it gets flagged and either updated or retired.

## 11. Meeting cadence

- **Monthly Council meeting:** 60 minutes, in person or Teams, last Friday of the month
- **Standing agenda:** review submissions, audit existing Plays, surface emerging student needs, assign next month's work
- **Quarterly AES advisor sync:** 60 minutes with Lamar, alignment on methodology and roadmap
- **Annual review (May):** transition handoff to incoming Council, retrospective, charter revisions if needed

## 12. Decision-making

- Routine editorial decisions: Chair has authority
- Publishing a new Play: majority Council vote
- Retiring or significantly rewriting a founding Play: Chair + AES advisor must concur
- Charter amendments: unanimous Council + AES advisor

## 13. Intellectual property & attribution

This section matters. Read it carefully.

- **The Miller AI Plays methodology** (Play schema, editorial standards, ETP integration where used) is and remains the intellectual property of Academic Enrichment Services.
- **The founding five Plays** authored by Lamar Martin remain his authored work, with permanent attribution.
- **Plays authored by Council members** are credited to the author by name, in perpetuity. Authors retain the right to cite their Plays on their CVs and in residency applications.
- **Aggregate Miller AI Plays content** (the library as a whole) is owned by AES on behalf of Miller School of Medicine, used under Council stewardship.
- **External use:** Council members may not publish Miller AI Plays content externally, license it to other institutions, or use it for commercial purposes without written AES approval. (AES retains the right to license the framework via GrayMar Strategies to other institutions.)
- **Research output:** Any research, conference presentation, or publication originating from Miller AI Plays is offered first to active Council members for co-authorship consideration based on contribution.

## 14. Removal and resignation

- Members may resign at any time with two weeks' notice
- Chair (with AES advisor concurrence) may remove a member for: failure to meet minimum contribution requirements, violation of academic integrity standards, or conduct unbecoming of the Council
- Removed or resigned members retain attribution on Plays they authored

## 15. Charter amendments

This Charter is a living document. Amendments require unanimous Council approval plus AES advisor concurrence. Amendments are version-controlled at the top of this document.
