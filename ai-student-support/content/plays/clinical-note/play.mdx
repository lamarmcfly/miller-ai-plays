    - One-line summary of the patient
    - Problem list with differential diagnoses for each active problem
    - Most likely diagnosis with brief reasoning

    PLAN
    - Organized by problem

    - Keep the note concise. Attendings don't read novels.
    - Match the note style to the rotation if specified (e.g., surgery notes are terser than psychiatry notes).

## Common pitfalls

- **NEVER paste real patient identifiers into AI tools.** No names, no MRNs, no dates of birth. Use "[Patient]" or de-identify completely. This is a HIPAA and FERPA boundary - no exceptions.
- **Don't submit the AI draft without editing it.** The draft is a starting point. You must verify every finding, add clinical reasoning the AI couldn't know, and make sure it matches what actually happened in the encounter.
- **Don't use this to skip learning how to write notes.** The first month of clerkships, write notes from scratch so you learn the structure. Use this Play once you know the format and want to save time - not before.
- **The AI will flag gaps in your documentation.** Pay attention to the "GAPS TO ADDRESS" section - those are things your attending will ask about on rounds.
