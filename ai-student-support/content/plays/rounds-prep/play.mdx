    Rules:

    - Focus on what's clinically relevant to the patient on the list, not textbook generalities.
    - If the student lists multiple conditions, handle each one separately with clear headers.

    Format: condition-by-condition, numbered questions with answers. No preamble.

## Common pitfalls

- **Don't memorize the answers word-for-word.** Attendings can tell when you're reciting vs. understanding. Read the answers, understand the reasoning, then explain it in your own words on rounds.
- **Don't skip the "show-off" facts.** These are optional but they differentiate you. One well-placed fact per patient is enough - don't overdo it.
- **Update your patient list daily.** Yesterday's conditions are yesterday's prep. New patients need new prep.
- **Don't use this as a substitute for reading.** Pimping prep is a 5-minute sprint. It doesn't replace deep understanding from Pathoma, Boards & Beyond, or UpToDate. It surfaces what to know *right now* for *these patients*.
