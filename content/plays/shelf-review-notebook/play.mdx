## How to set it up

This Play has more setup than the others, but you only do it once per shelf.

### Step 1 - Open NotebookLM
Go to **notebooklm.google.com** and sign in with your Google account.

### Step 2 - Create the notebook
Click **+ Create new notebook** and name it `Shelf - [Subject]` (e.g., `Shelf - Internal Medicine`).

### Step 3 - Load sources
NotebookLM accepts up to 50 sources per notebook. Curate carefully - quality over quantity.

**Strong sources:** your own notes, OnlineMedEd transcripts, high-yield review docs, clerkship handbooks, past shelf practice explanations you've curated.

**Do NOT load:** copyrighted Qbank content (NBME, UWorld), random web pages, personal patient notes.

### Step 4 - Start asking questions
Use the six prompt patterns from the Prompt section above. Every answer cites which source it came from.

## Common pitfalls

- **Don't load 50 random web pages.** NotebookLM's quality is bounded by your sources. Load 10 great sources, not 50 mediocre ones.
- **Don't upload copyrighted Qbank content.** NBME, UWorld, AMBOSS - their terms prohibit redistribution. Use your OWN notes from those resources instead.
- **Don't share notebooks with patient information.** FERPA + HIPAA. Even de-identified patient stories in your notes should be scrubbed before loading.
- **Don't treat citations as ground truth.** NotebookLM cites which source it pulled from, but it doesn't verify the source itself is correct. If your sources have errors, the notebook will repeat them.
- **Build one notebook per shelf, not one mega-notebook.** Cross-subject notebooks dilute the focus.
